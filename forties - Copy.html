Top Comedy Movies of 1940s

Bringing up baby

There’s real kinship in the infectious, rapid-fire dialogue and class critiques of the screwball genre, at least as rendered by masters like Frank Capra, Howard Hawks, Preston Sturges and others. Screwball comedies helped defuse Depression-era tensions and anxieties, both external and internal, and set the standard for witty repartee. The script, acting, and astonishingly deft pacing of Hawks’ Bringing Up Baby, which features irresistible performances from Katherine Hepburn and Cary Grant, gives it a leg up on nearly all others. His girl friday

His Girl Friday

Easily one of the funniest film ever made this screwball comedy features Cary Grant as a powerful news editor who is about to use his protege and ex-wife (Rosalind Russell) to a second marriage. He rapidly devises a scheme to distract the normally sauvy woman from her rapidly approaching nuptial. The picture became known for its rapid-fire dialogue and playful comedy. It is judged as the standard for the screwball comedy genre and has ever been noted as one of the earliest successful dramedies of all time..

The great dictator

The great Dictator

Years ahead of America's involvement in World War II, iconic movie star Charlie Chaplin delivered one of the finest performance of his career in this 1940 anti-war comedy satire Charlie Chaplin pulled a double duty as both a war veteran/jewish barber and a brutal fascist dictator hellbent on spreading his anti-semitic beliefs. Chaplin wrote, dictated, produced and starred in this Oscar-nominated film which is deemed as one of the greatest and most historically significant comedies ever created.

The Philadelphia Story

The philadelphia story

Katherine Hepburn stars as a socialite caught in a messy love triangle in this uproarious 1940 romantic comedy. Impending nuptial of this wealthy young woman becomes complicated with the arrival of her dashing ex-husband (Cary Grant) alongside a gossip magazine reporter(James Stewart) Hepburn's character was based on the life and antics of famed Philadelphia socialiteHelen Hope Montgomery Scott. The amusing flick won two academy awards including Best actor for James Stewart

Adam's Rib

Adam's Rib

Adverized as hilarious answer to "Who wears the pants", 1949's Adam's Rib starred Katherine Hepburn and Spencer Tracy as a married couple pitted against each other in the courtroom as opposing counsel. This early battle of sexes was inspired by the real life case of William and Dorothy Whitney. The film was nominated for both the Academy Awards and the Golden Globes.
